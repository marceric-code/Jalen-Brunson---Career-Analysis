# Jalen-Brunson---Career-Analysis

## Project Overview

This is an interactive Power BI dashboard that tracks Jalen Brunson's career statistics from his rookie season in 2018–19 all the way through 2024–25. It covers regular season totals, per-game averages, three-point shooting trends, and playoff performance — giving a full picture of how much he's grown as a player.
## Tools Used
- **StatMuse** — data source for career statistics
- **Microsoft Excel** — data cleaning and formatting
- **Power BI** — dashboard design, cleaning, and visualization

## The Data

All statistics were pulled from StatMuse and cleaned in Excel before being loaded into Power BI. The cleaning process was pretty straightforward — formatting columns consistently, making sure season labels were uniform, and removing any totals rows that would have thrown off the visuals.

## What's on the Dashboard

**KPI Cards** — Career totals and per-game averages for points, assists, rebounds, and steals at a glance.

**Points by Year** — A line chart showing how his scoring has grown season by season. The jump from his Dallas years to his first season in New York is pretty impressive.

**Points Overview** — A bar chart ranking each season by total points scored. The 2023–24 season stands out as his best by a wide margin.

**3PM by Season** — A bar chart tracking three-pointers made each season. You can see his range and confidence from distance growing year after year.

**Playoff Points & Assists** — A dual line chart comparing his points and assists across postseason appearances. This one tells an interesting story about how he's stepped up when it matters most

**Personal Info Panel** — A quick reference card with his position, number, age, height, weight, college, draft info, experience, and accolades

## Key Takeaways

- His 2023–24 season was his best statistically — **2,200+ points**, which ranked among the top scorers in the entire league that year.
- His three-point volume has grown significantly since coming to New York — **211 three-pointers made in 2023–24** compared to 63 in his rookie year.
- In the playoffs he's shown up — his points and assists both trend upward in his most recent postseason runs, which is exactly what you want from your franchise player.
- He went from a second-round pick at #33 to a 2x All-Star and 2x All-NBA selection — the numbers back up every bit of the recognition he has received.
